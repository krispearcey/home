      I’m a front-end developer based in Atlantic Canada. This site isn’t meant to be polished portfolio; it should act as more as a personal concoction of the projects, ideas, and rabbit holes that occupy my time.

      I have a particular weakness for over-complicated minimalism—the kind of "simple" design that actually requires a dozen hidden moving parts to feel seamless. Whether I'm building custom chess tools, experimenting with web layouts, or tinkering with general dev projects, I like taking a basic concept and pushing it until it feels distinctive.

      When I'm not staring at a code editor, I’m usually trying to get as far away from a screen as possible. Whether that's hitting the trails or finding a quiet spot for some fly fishing.

      Outside of that, I usually have a few half-finished projects open at once, follow ideas wherever they go, and occasionally turn those rabbit holes into something tangible. This page is meant to be a small record of those interests — what I’m making, what I’m reading, and what’s currently holding my attention.
